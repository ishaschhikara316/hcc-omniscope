About This Research

Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) thrives in hypoxic conditions by activating oxygen-sensing pathways originally shaped by millennia of human altitude adaptation. This research identifies two LASSO-Cox prognostic signatures: a 9-gene altitude adaptation set (C-index 0.671) and an 11-gene ROS/ferroptosis set (C-index 0.700), validated across four independent cohorts totaling 648 patients. HMOX1 bridges both signatures, linking evolutionary oxygen sensing to tumor redox biology.
